New Hampshire State Police History

A proud tradition of protecting and serving the Granite State

1937

Foundation Era

The New Hampshire State Police was established to provide law enforcement services across the state's highways and rural communities, setting the foundation for decades of dedicated service.

1996

Expansion & Integration

The division absorbed the Gaming Enforcement unit and State Hospital Security, incorporating 55 police officers and expanding capabilities to better serve New Hampshire communities.

2008

Highway Patrol Merger

The 80 officers of the New Hampshire Highway Patrol merged into the State Police, creating a unified force of approximately 380 troopers serving the entire state.

Today

Modern Era

The New Hampshire State Police continues to evolve with modern technology and training while maintaining the core values of integrity, professionalism, and community service that have defined the force since its inception.

Never Forgotten

We are committed to supporting memorial initiatives and ensuring that the sacrifices of these heroes are never forgotten. Through fundraising efforts and community support, we work to maintain memorials and provide assistance to the families of fallen officers.
